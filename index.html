GOVERNMENT TERTIARY FINANCIAL ASSISTANCE PROGRAM

Municipality of Tangalan, Aklan

📘 What is this program?

The GTFAP is a scholarship assistance program of the Municipal Government of Tangalan that provides financial support to qualified and deserving Tangalanon college students studying within the Province of Aklan.

  • Support students from low-income families
  • Promote access to college education
  • Help achieve at least one professional per family
  • Encourage academic excellence among Tangalanon youth
💰 Assistance Amount
₱4,000

PER SEMESTER

₱8,000

PER ACADEMIC YEAR

Renewable every semester subject to compliance with requirements.

🎯 Who Can Apply?
  • Natural-born Filipino
  • Resident of Tangalan for at least 3 years
  • Tangalanon college students in their second year or higher
  • Enrolled in a college within the Province of Aklan
  • With no grade lower than 75%
  • Family income not exceeding ₱130,000 annually
  • Applicant and parent/guardian are registered voters
  • Not a recipient of any other scholarship
  • Not yet a college graduate
  • Passed the Scholarship Qualifying Exam
❌ Who are not qualified?
  • Already receiving another scholarship
  • Have failing grades
  • Not residents of Tangalan / Enrolled outside Aklan
  • Family income above ₱130,000 annually
  • Already graduates of any degree
👨‍👩‍👧 LIMITATION: Only one (1) member per immediate family may avail of the scholarship.
📋 Requirements for Application
  • Accomplished Application Form
  • 2pcs 2x2 ID Picture
  • Bio Data
  • Photocopy of Report Card/Grades
  • Certificate of Good Moral Character from the school Principal, college Dean/President
  • Barangay Certification of Indigency
  • Voter’s Certificate (Applicant & Parent/Guardian)
MSWDO Certification: Specifically required for PWD or Solo Parent applicants to avail of priority status.
📚 To Continue Scholarship
  • Maintain grades of 75% or higher
  • Carry regular academic load (Not drop subjects)
  • Submit grades every semester
  • Participate in community service activities
  • Plant at least 5 seedlings per semester
  • Optional: Blood donation to MHO/PRC
